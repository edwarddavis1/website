An Introduction to Graph Embedding

You may not realise it, but graphs are everywhere. The neuron interactions in your brain provide you with conscious thought [ref], and protein interactions make up your immune system [ref]. The molecules that you interact with look [ref] and even smell [ref] in a specific way due to the structure of their atomic composition and the language you use to communicate holds meaning due to specific orderings and contexts of words [ref]. While each of these complex systems may seem to exist in wildy different domains, they can all be represented as a graph.

Graph embedding gives us a way of representing these large and complex systems in a concise space, allowing us to visualise the structure they hold as well as apply downstream machine learning techniques to learn about how these systems work.
